# User Manual — Campus Resource Sharing System

## Getting Started

### Creating an account
1. Go to the CRSS login page and click **Create an account**.
2. Fill in your full name, campus email, a password (minimum 8 characters), and select
   your role (Student, Faculty, or Club/Department).
3. Optionally add your department, course, year of study, and student ID — these help
   other users know who they're borrowing from or lending to.
4. Click **Create account**, then sign in. A verification email is sent to your address;
   click the link inside it to verify your account.

### Signing in
Enter your campus email and password on the login page. If you forget your password,
use the **Forgot password?** link to receive a reset email.

## Browsing and Borrowing

### Finding a resource
1. Go to **Browse Resources** in the sidebar.
2. Use the search box to look for a keyword (title, description, or tag), or narrow
   results with the category and condition filters.
3. Click any resource card to see full details, photos, owner info, and reviews.

### Requesting to borrow
1. On a resource's detail page, choose your desired **From** and **Until** dates.
2. Optionally explain your purpose (helps the owner decide faster).
3. Click **Send borrow request**. The owner is notified immediately (in-app, and by
   email if they have notifications enabled).
4. Track your request under **Borrow Requests → My requests**. You can cancel it any
   time before it's returned, as long as it hasn't already been marked returned.

### Returning a resource
Once your request shows status **Approved**, go to **Borrow Requests → My requests**
and click **Mark as returned** once you've physically handed the item back. If
anything was damaged, note it — this flags the request for the owner's attention rather
than silently closing it out.

### Leaving a review
After a borrow is marked returned, go back to the resource's page and you'll be able to
leave a star rating and comment — this only unlocks once you've actually completed a
borrow of that specific item.

## Lending Your Own Resources

### Listing a resource
1. Go to **Browse Resources → List a resource**.
2. Fill in a clear title and description, pick a category and condition, set how many
   units you have, your pickup location, and how many days someone can borrow it for at
   most.
3. An optional deposit amount can be set for higher-value items — this is tracked for
   accountability, not processed as a real payment.
4. Click **Publish resource**. You can add photos afterward from the resource's page.

### Managing incoming requests
Go to **Borrow Requests → Incoming requests** to see who wants to borrow your items.
Click **Approve** or **Reject** (with an optional reason) on each pending request.

## Notifications
The bell icon in the top bar shows your unread count and updates live while you're using
the app (no need to refresh). Click it to see the full list, and click any notification
to mark it read, or use **Mark all read**.

## Your Profile
Go to **My Profile** to update your name, department, course, year, bio, skills, and
phone number, or to change your password. Your **trust score** and **sharing score**
(visible on your dashboard) reflect your reliability as a borrower/lender over time.

## Filing a Complaint
If something goes wrong with a borrow (a user is unresponsive, an item was
misrepresented, etc.), use the complaint system to notify campus admins, who can review
and respond to your report.
